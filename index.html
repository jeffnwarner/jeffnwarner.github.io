Projects

Lucid You

This is an Android App built in Android Studio written in Java. The app is designed to help induce lucid dreaming in users through a frequency generator. The generated frequency should theoretically resonate with the users brain frequencies at a level that makes it more likely to have lucid dreams. The app also includes a dream journal for users to quickly write down the dreams that they had to better remember them later. There is also a training feature for the users in the form of a notification system. The training consists of the app repeatedly giving the users notifications throughout the day so they can condition themselves to ask the question "Am I dreaming?" Training the user to ask this question is another way to help them realize that they are dreaming while asleep and will help them dream lucidly. The dream journal can sync to a Firebase database and can also be seen on our website for users who wish to write their journals on their computers instead of their phones.

Cops and Robbers

At VICTR Lab one of the projects I worked on was a video game made in Unity. This video game is a "first-person shooter" with the purpose of it being used in a social experiment on people's thoughts on gun control. Players will answer a survey on their opinions and then play through the game and take the survey again to see if their opinions were affected at all after being in a virtual environment. For this project I worked on designing the level the player walked around in by taking a building asset from the Unity Asset Store and rearranging it so that the player and enemies could better hide behind shelves. I also helped program many functions the player had such as shooting the gun and crouching. As well as many scripts for player feedback to being shot such as adding blood to the screen when the player is shot as well as indicating to the player where the shot came from.
