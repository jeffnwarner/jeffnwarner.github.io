Projects

Lucid You

This is an Android App built in Android Studio written in Java.
The app is designed to help induce lucid dreaming in users through a frequency generator.
The app also includes a dream journal for users to quickly write down the dreams that they had to better remember them later.
There is also a training feature for the users in the form of a notification system.
The training consists of the app repeatedly giving the users notifications throughout the day so they can condition themselves to ask the question "Am I dreaming?"
Training the user to ask this question is another way to help them realize that they are dreaming while asleep and will help them dream lucidly.
The dream journal can sync to a Firebase database and can also be seen on our website for users who wish to write their journals on their computers instead of their phones.

Cops and Robbers

At VICTR Lab one of the projects I worked on was a video game made in Unity.
This video game is a "first-person shooter" that was used in the study "Personality Traits and Perceptions About Guns Affect the Relationship Between Violence Justification and Guilt."
This study measured the perception of violence justification through two different scenarios.
Participants were randomly selected to play out the scenario of either a police officer being attacked by robbers, or the scenario of a robber getting surrounded by police officers.
Participants would then answer survey questions to recount whether they believed that their actions of violence were justified depending on the scenario they were given.
For this project I worked on designing the level the player walked around in by taking a building asset from the Unity Asset Store and rearranging it so that the player and enemies could better hide behind shelves.
I also helped program many functions the player had such as shooting the gun and crouching.
As well as many scripts for player feedback to being shot such as adding blood to the screen when the player is shot as well as indicating to the player where the shot came from.

This social media app is designed with victims of domestic abuse in mind.
For the most part, it is a Twitter like clone where users can make posts and comment on posts, but the users are all anonymous to protect the users identities in case unwanted people find the app.
There is also a heat map of locations of public resoures for users to find. The main component that I worked on was the social media aspect of it.
